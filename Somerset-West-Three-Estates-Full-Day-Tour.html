Somerset West Three Estates Full Day Tour

Discover three prime historic wine estates of Somerset West, located less than one hour away from the city of Cape Town. This original Cape settler destination is one of the oldest wine producing regions of the Cape. Like many other winelands regions of the Cape, this land formed part of various pockets of lands that was distributed to the earliest Cape Dutch to the Western Cape. The Governor of the Cape at the time, Simon van der Stel made his mark in this region after his discovery of the ideal agricultural value and superior wine terroir of the Helderberg and Schaapenberg Mountains. Viticulture and botany history was made as 1820 settlers arrived on these mountain slopes, where magnificent homesteads were built and fine agricultural and wine making traditions are passed on to new generations today. Come and enjoy the rich cultural heritage of this unique wine region.

Wine Estates

Lourensford Wine Estate (1700) – This estate is steeped in history, and continues to producing superior wine, coffee roasting and tastings, a home bakery, delicatessen, all amid a spectacular mountain setting.

Morgenster Wine Estate (1711) – Explore one of South Africa’s most successful olive and olive oil producers of export quality and awards. Taste local cheeses, olives, varieties of extra virgin olive oil, olive pastes, and wine tasting. Morgenster has received international acclaim for its Bordeaux style wines. 

Vergelegen Estate (1685) - cellar tour, wine tasting, 18 gardens, ancient Camphor and Yellowwood trees, historic homestead, slave lodge site, and library tour. Special cellar tour for architectural interest by arrangement. Explore the estate’s unique vintages in the three extra ordinary ranges: Flagship, Reserve and Premium ranges.

Lunch at Vergelegen – Discover the new from the old at the modern day Stables Restaurant and Bistro, with views across the Helderberg mountains and Schaapenberg. Enjoy a leisurely stroll in any one of the 18 gardens on the estate, where during the winter months the Japonica Camellia garden is in full bloom.

Tour Route

Travel along the coastal Indian Ocean route via Stellenbosch to Somerset West. Return to along the N2 freeway, past the local townships of Langa and Guguletu, the University of Cape Town, Ou Kaapse weg for views of the Cape Floral Kingdom. Upon return you may pass the False Bay village of Fish Hoek, and location of the historic archeological site at Peers Cave, en route to the Far South Peninsula with views of the Atlantic Ocean and Kommetjie Lighthouse.
